Bridges

One Science Olympiad event I’m in is Bridge. I spent a lot of time perfecting these bridges. The purpose of this event is to build the lightest wooden bridge that can still hold the whole weight of 15,000g. I used a combination of balsa (compression members) and basswood (tension members)

By testing out different designs, making tension members very thin, only having bracing in the more critical spots, and finding the optimal density for each wood piece, I was able to make a 9.6g bridge that held 15,000g. In terms of weight, that would be around 2 pieces of paper holding up two bicycles. (And, big shout out to my teammates who always help me test my bridges!)

SoloStep

In collaboration with MIT, The Lemelson Foundation sponsors Lemelson-MIT InvenTeams™, where high school students, educators, and mentors come together to tackle real-world challenges with grants of up to $7,500.

Our EMUiNVENT InvenTeam was 1 of 8 teams nationwide selected to receive this grant, allowing us to develop a tech-driven invention that addresses local issues. The problem we picked was mobility issues. With the U.S. elderly population expected to double to 95 million by 2060, seniors are increasingly facing challenges in maintaining mobility and independence, leading to a range of health risks and psychological impacts. Many older adults rely on others for basic movement and positional changes, which reduces physical activity and results in medical consequences, such as heart disease, obesity, and elevated blood pressure, as well as increased caregiver costs and mental health effects like depression and loneliness.

We created a mini, fully-functioning prototype and worked with an outside fabricator (Mr. Chris Trager) to help us assemble a limited-functioning, full scale prototype.

Music for Seniors & Tech Town Hall

I co-founded Music for Seniors, which is a group that performs at retirement homes (StoryPoint and University Living) twice a month during the school year and weekly in the summer.

When interviewing seniors about their mobility issues in the EMUiNVENT InvenTeam, we learned that they would appreciate some help with technology. This is why I also co-founded Tech Town Hall at the Saline Senior Center. We meet every month to help seniors with tech-related inquiries and to help them set up things.
